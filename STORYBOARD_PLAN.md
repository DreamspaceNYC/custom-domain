## 2) Visual Style Bible

- **Format**: 16:9 widescreen, high detail, cinematic true-crime documentary reenactment.
- **Lens language**: mix of 24mm establishing, 35mm conversational, 50–85mm emotional closeups; shallow depth of field.
- **Lighting**: warm tungsten indoors for domestic scenes; cold blue/green night tones for tension/crime scenes; motivated practicals (lamps, streetlights, police lights).
- **Color palette**: muted neutrals, desaturated skin tones, restrained contrast, occasional red/blue emergency reflections.
- **Mood**: suspense, unease, procedural realism.
- **Safety constraints baked into every prompt**:
  - no graphic gore, no blood, no visible wounds
  - no explicit bullet impact
  - violent moments shown by implication only (shadows, reactions, objects, silhouettes)
  - fictionalized generic faces (no real likeness)
  - no readable text, no logos, no official insignia, no station/department names
